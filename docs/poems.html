Henry David Thoreau (1817-1862)

  • I Was Born Upon Thy Bank, River

    I was born upon thy bank, river, My blood flows in thy stream, And thou meanderest forever At the bottom of my dream

John Fletcher (1579-162)

  • The River-God's Song

    Do not feat to they feet Naked in the river sweet; Think not leech or newt or toad Will bite thy foot when thou hast trod; Nor let the water rising high As thou wad'st in make thee cry And sob, but ever live with me, And not a wave shall trouble thee.
